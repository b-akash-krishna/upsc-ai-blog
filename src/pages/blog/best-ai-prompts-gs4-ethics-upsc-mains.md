GS4 is the paper that confuses almost every aspirant. You read Lexicon cover to cover, memorise every thinker, and then in the exam you write 250 words of theory — and still score 95 out of 250.

The problem isn't your knowledge. It's how you're *applying* it.

Ethics answers need to be personal, nuanced, and rooted in real-life examples. That combination is hard to practice alone. AI — with the right prompts — can help you crack exactly that.

## What Makes a Good GS4 Answer?

Before the prompts, understand what UPSC actually wants:

- **Philosophical grounding** — Reference to thinkers like Kant, Aristotle, Gandhi, or Rawls
- **Personal integrity angle** — Not just theory, but what *you* would do as a civil servant
- **Case study thinking** — Practical ethical dilemmas with structured decision-making
- **Balanced view** — Acknowledging competing values (duty vs compassion, rules vs outcomes)

Most aspirants write only the first point. The rest gets ignored. That's where AI helps.

## Prompt 1: Generate a Multi-Framework Analysis

> *"I am answering this UPSC GS4 question: [paste question]. Analyse this ethical issue using at least 3 frameworks: Kantian ethics, Utilitarian approach, and Virtue Ethics. Then suggest what a civil servant with integrity and compassion would do. Keep it under 200 words."*

## Prompt 2: Find Relevant Thinkers and Quotes

> *"For a UPSC Ethics answer on [topic], suggest 3–4 relevant philosophers or thinkers with a one-line quote or idea from each that I can naturally include. Also suggest one Indian thinker like Gandhi, Ambedkar, or Kautilya."*

## Prompt 3: Improve a Case Study Answer

> *"Here is my answer to a UPSC Ethics case study: [paste answer]. Evaluate it on these criteria: identification of ethical issues, stakeholder analysis, options considered, best course of action with justification, and constitutional values. Give specific suggestions for each criterion."*

## Prompt 4: Add the Personal Integrity Dimension

> *"My answer to this ethics question lacks a personal/civil servant dimension. Suggest 2–3 sentences I can add that reflect integrity, empathy, and commitment to public service without sounding hollow or bookish."*

## Prompt 5: Practice Aptitude Questions

> *"Give me 5 UPSC-style GS4 aptitude questions on [topic]. For each question, also give a model answer structure so I can practice writing it myself."*
