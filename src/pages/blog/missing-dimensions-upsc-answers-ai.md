"Your answer lacks dimensions." Every UPSC aspirant has heard this. But what does it actually mean — and how do you fix it systematically?

## What Are "Dimensions" in UPSC?

A dimension is any angle or perspective through which an issue can be analysed:

- **Social** — Impact on communities, marginalized groups, gender
- **Economic** — GDP, employment, inequality, fiscal impact
- **Political** — Governance, federalism, electoral implications
- **Environmental** — Climate, biodiversity, sustainability
- **Legal/Constitutional** — Relevant articles, court judgments
- **Historical** — Root causes, colonial legacy, evolution
- **International** — Global comparisons, treaties, geopolitics
- **Ethical** — Moral dimensions, civil servant values

A 15-mark answer typically needs 4–5 of these. Most aspirants cover 2–3.

## The AI Dimension Checker Prompt

After writing any answer, use this immediately:

> *"I wrote the following UPSC Mains answer on [topic]. Identify every important dimension I have MISSED — political, economic, social, legal, constitutional, environmental, or gender angle. For each missing dimension, write 2 sentences on what I should have included. Indian context only. My answer: [paste answer]"*

Within seconds you'll see exactly what the examiner expected and didn't find.

## Why This Is a Game-Changer

Most aspirants don't know what they don't know. The dimension checker makes your blind spots visible. After using it for 2 weeks, you'll start *anticipating* dimensions before you even write — because you've seen the pattern repeatedly.

## Building a Dimension Checklist

After a week of using the AI checker, create a personal checklist of your 3 most commonly missed dimensions. Before submitting any practice answer, run through that checklist manually.

This two-step process — AI checker + personal checklist — is more effective than any coaching feedback session.
