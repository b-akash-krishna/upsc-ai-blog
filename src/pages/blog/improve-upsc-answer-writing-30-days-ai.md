Thirty days is enough time to transform your answer writing — if you're systematic. Here's the exact week-by-week plan.

## Week 1: Diagnose Your Baseline

Write 5 answers on different GS topics. Evaluate each using:

> *"Act as a UPSC Mains examiner. Score this answer out of 15 — content (6), structure (5), language (4). List my top 3 weaknesses specifically."*

By Day 7: Write down your top 3 recurring weaknesses. These become your focus for the next 3 weeks.

## Week 2: Fix Structure

**Daily:** Write 1 answer, then use:

> *"Evaluate the structure of this answer only. Is the introduction, body, and conclusion clearly demarcated? Score out of 10 for structure with specific reasoning."*

**Weekend:** Rewrite your weakest answer from the week based on structural feedback.

## Week 3: Add Dimensions and Depth

**Daily:** After writing an answer, use the dimension checker:

> *"Which dimensions are present and absent: social, economic, political, environmental, ethical, historical, international? For each absent dimension, give 2 specific points I can add."*

## Week 4: Polish Language and Punch

**Daily:**

> *"Identify sentences in my answer that are vague or unnecessarily complex. Rewrite them to be more direct and impactful."*

**Final weekend:** Write a timed mock — 5 questions in 3 hours. Compare AI evaluation to Week 1's scores.

## What to Expect

Most aspirants who do this consistently report noticeably stronger introductions, answers that feel "complete," and a clearer sense of what UPSC rewards. The improvement is real, measurable, and compounds over the next 3–6 months.
