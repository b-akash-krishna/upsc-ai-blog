This is a genuine question worth answering honestly — not just with AI hype.

## What Coaching Feedback Does Well

- Human intuition about what examiners respond to emotionally
- Mentor's personal experience of what worked in their own attempt
- Accountability — you're less likely to skip practice if someone is checking
- Nuanced feedback on writing style and personality in answers

## What AI Feedback Does Better

- **Speed:** Instant vs 3–5 days
- **Availability:** 24/7 vs office hours
- **Consistency:** Same rigorous criteria every time, no off days
- **Dimensional analysis:** AI systematically checks every dimension; humans often focus on what they know best
- **Cost:** Free vs ₹500–₹2000 per session
- **Volume:** Evaluate 10 answers per day; no mentor can do this

## Where AI Falls Short

- Can't replicate the "feel" of an actual UPSC examiner
- May miss subtle contextual nuances in very current affairs-heavy questions
- Doesn't know *your* specific weaknesses over time (unless you build a system for it)

## The Verdict

For **daily practice and self-evaluation**: AI is superior in almost every dimension.

For **1–2 major reviews per month** on your best answers: Human feedback from an experienced mentor or topper is still valuable.

The optimal approach: AI for 90% of your practice. Human feedback 1–2 times a month to calibrate.

This combination costs a fraction of full coaching and produces better results for most self-motivated aspirants.
