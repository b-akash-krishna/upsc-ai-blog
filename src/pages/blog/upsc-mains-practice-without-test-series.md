A good UPSC test series costs ₹15,000–₹40,000. Feedback is delayed by days. And you get one evaluator's opinion — not a structured, multi-dimensional analysis.

AI gives you unlimited practice, instant feedback, and costs nothing.

## Building Your Own Practice System

### Daily Practice (30 minutes)

1. Generate a question: *"Give me one UPSC Mains GS[1/2/3/4] question on [today's topic]. 15 marks, 250 words."*
2. Write your answer in 15 minutes (timed)
3. Evaluate using the examiner prompt
4. Note one weakness to fix tomorrow

### Weekly Mock (2 hours)

> *"Create a 5-question UPSC Mains practice set for GS[paper] covering different sub-topics. Mix 10-mark and 15-mark questions. Present as an exam paper."*

Write all 5 answers timed. Then evaluate all 5 together.

### Monthly Progress Test

At the end of each month, write the same 3 questions you wrote in Week 1. Compare the evaluation scores. The improvement will be visible and motivating.

## Why This Beats a Test Series

- **Instant feedback** vs 3–5 day wait
- **Unlimited questions** vs fixed question sets
- **Specific dimensional analysis** vs generic "add more examples"
- **Available at 2 AM** vs office hours only
- **Free** vs ₹15,000–₹40,000

The only thing a test series has over AI is the experience of writing on paper under exam conditions. Replicate that yourself by printing questions and writing by hand for your monthly mock.
